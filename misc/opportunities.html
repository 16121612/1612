Technology Opportunities

The page or area describing the opportunities presented by your chosen technology/topic.

    Less incentives to owning vehicles.
The average cost of owning a car is estimated to be around $8,000 per annual. Needless to say, it is costly to own and maintain a personal vehicle as compared to public transportation which is 3x cheaper. However, this problem could easily be a thing of the past with the addition of driverless AI vehicles. Ride share services like Uber have invested well over 1 billion dollars into autonomous vehicles, which will produce a fleet of driverless vehicles. Without the need of human drivers to pay, the operational costs of such a fleet would drastically decrease in price for customers, providing people with an alternative to driving personal vehicles. Getting people to their destinations more efficiently with the high possibility of reducing number of vehicles driven has many indirect environmental impacts. It is good to remember that the less fuel burned means in less fuel sold, which ultimately results in a less demanding need for fossil fuel resources and the many emission-heavy processes used to extract the fossil fuels. And if this positive trend continues in our society, we can soon start seeing people using ride-sharing services more than purchasing their own vehicles, eventually fewer cars needed to be manufactured, which reduces the impact of that process as well. Another thing to note is that the more hours a vehicle is used through car-sharing, any related up-front battery costs could be shared, also noting that it does not require passengers to figure out a place to park. Increasing the economic appeal of AI driven vehicles. All of these factors can very potentially spur a higher demand for vehicle-sharing, also known as car-pooling.
    Reduced Congestion
The price difference is not the only thing to entice people, along with AI driven vehicles comes reduced traffic congestion. It is also good to note that there is various of different factors that might cause traffic congestions, car crashes/accidents and multiple stop-and-go traffic waves that often causes clusters of congestions. This can be addressed with AI driven vehicles, AI are able to maintain a safe and consistent distance between vehicles, allowing to reduce accidents caused by human error and the number of stop-and-go waves that produce traffic congestions. These AI advancements also stand to have a direct impact on the environment by reducing the number of vehicles on the road and helping those that remain get where they are going more efficiently. Even features like collision avoidance accomplish this indirectly: Fewer accidents means less traffic congestion and fewer emergency vehicle dispatches. Reduced number of traffic congestions means saving fuel and ultimately reducing greenhouse gases from needless vehicle idling. Public transportation accounts for more than one quarter of greenhouse gas emissions in the U.S., according to the studies at EPA. It is not just about convenience, but our environment as well.
